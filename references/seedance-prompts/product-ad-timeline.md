# Product Ad Timeline Seedance Prompt Pattern

Use this pattern for concise 12-20 second product ads driven by a storyboard image or product/brand board.

## Best For

- fitness equipment
- vehicles
- devices
- beauty products
- luxury goods
- product hero films
- high-density commercial timelines

## Core Structure

Include:

- product/category
- environment
- style
- camera language
- sound
- scene-by-scene timeline
- end frame / logo frame

## Timeline Rules

Use short scenes:

- 1-1.5 seconds for quick commercial beats
- 2-3 seconds for hero/product reveal
- macro closeups for material and function
- controlled camera movement
- product and human use clearly shown

## Reusable Seedance Prompt Structure

```text
Create a cinematic [duration]-second product ad for [product/brand].

STYLE:
[photorealistic / high contrast / premium / color palette / grain / bloom]

CAMERA:
controlled cinematic movement: low-angle, push-in, tracking, macro, lateral slide, realistic motion blur.

SOUND:
[bass / breath / mechanical motion / product sound / ambient tone]

SCENE 1 (0:00-0:01.5):
[wide establish / aspirational image]

SCENE 2:
[human/hero subject close-up]

SCENE 3:
[material/action detail]

SCENE 4:
[product hero reveal]

SCENE 5:
[user beside/with product]

SCENE 6:
[product in use, function visible]

SCENE 7:
[human performance/reaction]

SCENE 8:
[feature detail]

SCENE 9:
[macro material/engineering]

SCENE 10:
[recovery/beauty moment]

SCENE 11:
[surface/material close-up]

SCENE 12:
[hero toward camera / final action]

SCENE 13:
[logo/end frame, minimal glow, fade out]
```

## Workflow Note

For high-quality results:

1. Upload product images.
2. Generate or refine a brand/product poster.
3. Iterate until product look is locked.
4. Generate a storyboard poster based on that product board.
5. Use the product board + storyboard in Seedance.

This is already mostly covered by `luxury-ad-creative-direction-board.md`; use this file for Seedance prompt phrasing.

## Common Negative Controls

Add these when useful:

- no subtitles
- no storyboard frames
- no poster layout
- no e-commerce short-video feeling
- no cheap studio look
- no over-warm filter unless requested
- no readable brand text unless final logo/title is explicitly required
- keep product appearance consistent
- keep model/actor identity consistent
