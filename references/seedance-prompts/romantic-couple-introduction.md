# Romantic Couple Introduction Seedance Prompt Pattern

Use this pattern when turning a couple character reference sheet into a short cinematic introduction scene.

## Best For

- couple introduction clips
- romance drama character intros
- dating-show style intros
- relationship reveal scenes
- soft emotional two-person scenes

## Core Intent

The video should make the couple feel believable and lived-in.

It reveals the relationship through:

- glances
- smiles
- teasing
- soft gestures
- small reactions
- shared laughter
- quiet intimate moments
- micro-expressions

## Required Reference Use

Use the provided couple character sheet as reference.

Do not treat the sheet as one static image. Use its elements as separate shots:

- face close-ups
- outfit/material details
- chemistry panels
- posture panels
- close-up couple pose
- full identity/scale view

## Structure

Use:

```text
detail -> identity -> relationship -> presence -> full reveal
```

## Couple Tone Variable

Let user choose or infer:

- romantic
- funny
- shy
- flirty
- passionate
- soft
- playful
- dramatic
- intimate

## Performance Rules

Make the characters active:

- they look into camera
- speak naturally or seem to introduce themselves
- move and react
- interact with their environment and each other
- use short natural gestures
- shift posture with purpose
- reveal their relationship through behavior, not exposition

Show acting range:

- confidence
- hesitation
- curiosity
- tenderness
- amusement
- affection
- intensity

Express through:

- micro-expressions
- eyes
- tone
- body language

## Camera And Lighting

Camera:

- controlled
- minimal movement
- soft push-ins
- light tracking
- subtle handheld

Lighting:

- cinematic and consistent
- match mood to couple tone

Ending:

- confident mid or full shot
- both characters fully established
- final shared moment that clearly shows the relationship

## Reusable Seedance Prompt

```text
Use the provided couple character sheet @[image1] as reference.

Create a cinematic couple introduction video.

COUPLE_TONE: [romantic / funny / shy / flirty / passionate / soft / playful / dramatic / intimate]

Open with the characters looking into camera and speaking naturally, introducing themselves in their own words. They should feel like two people who are in love, with clear chemistry, warmth, and personality.

Do not treat the sheet as a single image. Use its elements as separate shots.

Structure:
detail -> identity -> relationship -> presence -> full reveal

Make the characters active: they move, react, interact with their environment and each other while talking. Use short natural gestures, small shifts, purposeful motion. Show their relationship through glances, smiles, teasing, gentle closeness, small disagreements, shared laughter, or quiet intimate moments.

Show acting range: subtle emotional shifts while speaking; confidence, hesitation, curiosity, tenderness, amusement, affection, intensity. Express through micro-expressions, eyes, tone, and body language.

Include face close-ups, outfit/material details, prop usage, expressive performance moments, couple interaction beats, and small reaction shots between them.

Keep everything grounded and realistic. The relationship should feel believable, natural, and lived-in, not exaggerated or artificial.

Camera: controlled minimal movement, soft push-ins, light tracking, subtle handheld.
Lighting: cinematic and consistent, matching the selected couple tone.

End on a confident mid or full shot, with both characters fully established. Finish with a final shared moment that clearly shows their relationship.
```
