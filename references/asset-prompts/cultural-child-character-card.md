# Cultural Child Character Card Pattern

Use this pattern when designing a child or teen character where cultural authenticity, height, body proportions, micro-performance, local wardrobe, and identity continuity matter.

This pattern is useful for AI live-action drama, animated family films, Southeast Asian / Chinese / regional stories, school-age characters, and any role that must not become a generic Westernized child.

## Best For

- child protagonists
- school-age characters
- culturally specific family/drama stories
- Southeast Asian / Chinese / regional identity roles
- animated film pitch boards with local authenticity
- multi-character scenes where height and body scale matter

## Core Intent

Create a film-production-grade character design sheet that feels like a premium animated feature pitch board, not a generic model sheet.

The character should feel like a real child actor captured mid-scene:

- curious eyes
- micro-expressions
- restless body rhythm
- imperfect posture
- emotion shifting quickly
- culturally grounded face, clothing, and behavior

## Cultural Authenticity Rules

When a character has a specific ethnicity, region, class background, or local culture:

- preserve that cultural identity in facial structure, skin tone, body language, wardrobe, hair, and accessories
- avoid default Westernized facial structure
- avoid generic “international kid” design
- use locally plausible clothes, fabric wear, shoes, school items, street dust, humidity, and everyday details
- make costume choices reflect family background, place, climate, and daily life

## Child Proportion Rules

For children:

- height must be clearly stated and shown with a scale marker
- head may be slightly larger for emotional readability, but still natural
- shoulders and limbs should reflect age
- hands can be slightly expressive but not adult-sized
- posture should not feel like an adult model pose
- clothing should show real child movement: stretched collar, loose fit, worn shoes, untied laces, pocket folds, sweat wrinkles

## Required Modules

Include:

- character identity: name, alias, age, height, build, ethnicity/design language
- body description: child proportions, shoulder/arm/leg traits, posture, movement rhythm
- face design: face shape, cheeks, nose, jaw, eyebrows, lips, skin texture, eye shape, hair
- distinctive features: scars, dimples, nervous habits, small asymmetries
- psychological profile: core traits, internal conflict, behavior patterns, emotional baseline
- performance direction: micro-expressions, restless gestures, transitional emotions
- wardrobe: local clothing logic, fabric stretch, dirt, sweat, stitching, worn shoes
- height scale: mandatory
- production notes: identity anchors, clothing logic, behavior habits, continuity locks

## Performance Direction

Ask for:

- lip tension
- eye flick movement
- subtle eyebrow asymmetry
- half-smiles
- nervous swallowing
- quick emotional transitions
- hands in pockets, scratching eyebrow, bouncing leg, looking around, fidgeting

Avoid:

- symmetrical staged posing
- expressionless child model
- adult-like stillness
- exaggerated meme expressions

## Reusable Instruction Block

```text
创建一张电影制作级儿童角色设计表，供导演、选角团队、服装部门、动画团队和 AI 视频团队使用。角色必须具有明确的地域文化真实感，不要默认生成西式面孔或泛国际化儿童。人物要像真实童星在叙事瞬间被捕捉到，而不是为概念图僵硬摆拍。

必须体现儿童真实比例、身高比例尺、略不稳定的体态、自然微表情、快速变化的情绪和本地生活痕迹。服装必须来自角色真实生活逻辑：当地气候、家庭背景、学校/街区环境、磨损、汗渍、灰尘、缝线、鞋底磨损和小道具都要合理。

构图必须像高预算动画电影 pitch board：艺术指导式轻微不对称布局、主次清楚、生产注释简洁专业。禁止普通等距网格、机械对称、通用模型表、廉价写真感。
```

## Continuity Labels

Use large labels:

- SAME CHILD IDENTITY
- KEEP CULTURAL FACE STRUCTURE
- KEEP HEIGHT SCALE
- KEEP CHILD PROPORTIONS
- KEEP LOCAL WARDROBE LOGIC
- NO WESTERNIZED FACE DRIFT
- NO ADULT BODY PROPORTIONS
- PERFORMANCE BASELINE
